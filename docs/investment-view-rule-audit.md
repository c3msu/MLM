# Investment View Rule Audit

Target surface: section 09, `ideas` in `the-dial-treasury-v1/data/dashboard.json`.

The investment-view cards are deterministic rules, not LLM output. They are
generated by `build_ideas()` from public-data indicators, macro Conditions
Score, and QRA fields, then rendered as editable cards in the browser.
Each card also receives `confidenceLevel`, `confidenceLabel`, and
`confidenceNote` from `conclusionAudit`, so the UI can distinguish directional
views from evidence quality.
Each card also receives an `equityImpact` block. This block is a historical
S&P 500 price-index proxy for SPY impact summary, not a forecast. It is
computed only from historical observations in `macroLiquidityEquity.series`
whose current Conditions Score bucket and 3-month score-change bucket match the
current environment.

## Inputs

| Rule block | Primary inputs | Feasibility gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Duration | CPI, PCE, core PCE, Dallas Fed Trimmed Mean PCE, PPI, 2Y one-month change, Conditions Score | Avoids adding duration unless inflation is cooling and 2Y is repricing lower. Keeps short duration when inflation is hot, 2Y sells off, Dallas Trimmed PCE confirms sticky underlying inflation, or macro conditions are tight. |
| 5s30s curve | 5s30s level, QRA next-quarter borrowing | Does not chase steepeners when the curve is already steep and QRA borrowing is light. Keeps or sizes down steepeners when supply is heavy but curve is already extended. |
| Front end | 2Y, EFFR, SOFR, 2Y one-month change | Downgrades carry when 2Y trades far below EFFR and has rallied sharply, because cuts are already priced. |
| Breakeven inflation | 10Y BEI, CPI/PCE/core PCE/Dallas Trimmed PCE, PPI, WTI, WTI shock | Long breakevens require hot inflation and either energy confirmation or not-rich BEI. Rich BEI with cooling inflation and soft energy is reduced. |

## Scenario Coverage

| Scenario | Expected section-09 behavior |
| --- | --- |
| Hot inflation, tight macro, heavy QRA | `SHORT 久期`, `CURVE 做陡`, `LONG 前端`, `RV 通胀`. |
| Disinflation, 2Y rally, loose macro | Duration switches to `LONG 久期`; breakeven drops to small hedge unless energy or inflation reheats. |
| Very steep 5s30s, light QRA | Curve view switches to `CURVE 观望` and explicitly says not to chase steepeners. |
| Front end already prices cuts | Front-end view switches to `FRONT-END 谨慎`; it is no longer described as a cash substitute. |
| Cooling inflation, soft oil, rich BEI | Breakeven view switches to `RV 降通胀补偿`. |

## Confidence Overlay

`conclusionAudit` remains the source of truth for evidence quality. The
investment-view cards copy a compact summary:

- `confidenceLevel`: `high`, `medium`, or `low`.
- `confidenceLabel`: Chinese display label such as `高可信`.
- `confidenceNote`: evidence quality, proxy/model share, single-factor
  concentration, and the audit's weight recommendation.

This overlay does not change trade direction by itself. It changes how strongly
the UI should present that direction and gives the reader an immediate warning
when a rule-triggered idea is built on weaker evidence.

## Historical SPY Impact Overlay

`investment_view_equity_impact()` attaches the same historical equity-impact
read-through to every section-09 card. The intent is to show how broad U.S.
equities behaved in past macro-liquidity states that looked similar, without
inventing a new causal claim for each trade.

Inputs and gates:

- Source: `macroLiquidityEquity.series`, backed by FRED `SP500` price-index
  history and the dashboard's own Conditions Score history.
- Match rule: current `levelBucket` plus current `changeBucket`, i.e. similar
  Conditions Score level and similar 3-month score momentum.
- Minimum sample: 6 matched historical observations. Below that, the UI shows
  `available: false` and a sample-insufficient explanation.
- Reported values: 1M/3M/6M median forward return, 3M hit rate, average 3M
  max drawdown, sample size, confidence, and a short summary.
- Language boundary: summaries must say `历史同类环境` or equivalent historical
  wording. They must not call the result a prediction.

This overlay is deliberately separate from the Treasury trade rule direction.
It can provide risk-context for SPY/SPX exposure, but it does not flip duration,
curve, front-end, or breakeven views.

If `content/overrides.json` supplies `ideas`, those manual cards replace the
generated section-09 payload as supplied. The API will not invent
`confidence*` or `equityImpact` fields for manual overrides. The browser still
shows a low-confidence placeholder for missing historical SPY impact so the
absence of generated statistics is visible.

## Remaining Boundaries

- These cards are rule-based research views, not portfolio sizing or trade
  execution advice.
- They do not yet use realized P/L, drawdown, carry/roll-down simulation, or
  transaction costs.
- They now display `conclusionAudit.confidence`, but low confidence does not
  automatically flip a view or resize a trade. It is a presentation and review
  guardrail, not a portfolio engine.
- The SPY impact module uses FRED `SP500` as a price-index proxy. It does not
  include SPY dividends, ETF trading frictions, intraday drawdowns, or regime
  causality beyond the observed historical matching rule.
- Dallas Fed Trimmed Mean PCE is treated as a policy-reaction-function watch
  item because it filters extreme component noise. The rule engine should not
  hard-code any individual policymaker's preference without an explicit public
  source; it should keep using the published data series as the observable.
- QRA thresholds are simple public-data gates. A more robust version should
  compare borrowing and refunding to historical percentiles after more QRA
  history is persisted.
